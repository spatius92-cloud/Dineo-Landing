A letter, long overdue

For Dineo

From Screw — with everything I have left in me

Read on

Introduction

I need you to hear me out

Dineo, I have started writing this more times than I can count. I'd get a few lines in, delete everything, and tell myself it was too late. That you had moved on. That I had no right. But something in me — the part that still knows what real feels like — kept bringing me back to this blank page. So here I am, finally.

I am not writing this because I am lonely or bored or running out of options. I am writing this because of you. Specifically you. Not a version of you I made up in my head, but the real Dineo — the one who laughed a certain way when something genuinely caught her off guard, the one who loved deeply and quietly, who showed up even when she didn't have to, who made me feel like I mattered even before I knew how to believe that.

I know five years is a long time. I know things have happened, life has moved, and the world has kept spinning in ways neither of us planned for. I am not pretending otherwise. But I also know that when something is real — truly, honestly real — time doesn't just erase it. It sits somewhere inside you and waits. And this has been sitting in me for a very long time.

I want you to read everything I've written here. Not because I expect anything, but because you deserve to know the truth — all of it — from someone who should have said it a long time ago. I owe you that, at the very least.

I am not the same person I was when I walked away. Not even close. And what I want now, more than anything, is for you to see that — if you'll let me show you.

So please, if you have the heart to, keep reading.

Apology

I was stupid. And I am so, so sorry.

There is no clever way to say this, so I'll just say it plainly: what I did five years ago was one of the dumbest things I have ever done in my life. I ended something rare. Something that most people search for their entire lives and never find. And I did it like it was nothing. Like you were nothing. And that is the part that kills me the most, because you were never nothing to me.

I was stupid, Dineo. Not in a small, forgivable way — I mean genuinely, embarrassingly, inexcusably stupid. We had something good. Something real. And instead of protecting it, instead of fighting for it, I let my own issues win. I let fear make that decision for me, dressed it up as something reasonable, and walked away like I was doing something brave. I wasn't. I was running.

I think about the way things ended between us, and it genuinely haunts me. Not in a dramatic, movie-scene way — I mean in the quiet, everyday kind of way. A song comes on. I pass a place. I hear someone laugh a certain way. And for just a second, it's you. And then comes that familiar weight in my chest that has never fully gone away.

You deserved better than what I gave you. You deserved someone who could stand in the love you were offering without flinching. Someone who could receive all of who you are without pulling back. You were that person for me, and I failed to be that for you. I am deeply, genuinely sorry for that.

I am not asking you to forget any of it. I am not asking you to pretend it didn't hurt. What I am asking is that you let my apology be what it is — honest, full, and long overdue. I am sorry for leaving. I am sorry for the silence that followed. I am sorry for every moment you had to carry something I put down.

You didn't deserve any of it. And I am sorry, Dineo. From the deepest part of me.

The Truth

What I know now — and wish I knew then

The honest truth is that your love scared me. Not because it was too much — it wasn't. It was actually exactly what I needed. But I didn't know how to hold something that big without dropping it. I didn't know how to be loved by someone who meant it, truly meant it, without feeling like I was going to lose myself in it. That was my problem. It had nothing to do with you.

I felt your love, Dineo. I want you to know that. I felt every single piece of it. The way you cared, the way you chose me, the way you saw me — really saw me, not the version I performed for the rest of the world, but the actual me — I felt all of it. And instead of being grateful, I panicked. I told myself stories about why it couldn't work, why I wasn't ready, why walking away made sense. None of those stories were true. I was just afraid.

I have spent the last five years doing the work I should have done before I even met you. I have looked at myself honestly — not just the parts I was comfortable with, but the parts I avoided. The patterns. The walls. The reasons I kept pushing people away before they could leave first. I understand now what I couldn't understand then. I know what a real relationship actually requires. Not perfection, not performance — just presence, honesty, and the willingness to stay even when it's hard.

And here is the other truth: in my whole life, no one has loved me the way you did. I have been with other people since you. I have tried to move on. But nothing has ever touched what we had. Not even close. Because what you gave me wasn't just affection — it was something rooted. Something real. And you can't fake your way to something like that with someone else.

I am not the same person I was. I am clearer now about who I am, what I want, and — more importantly — how to show up for the person I love. That person is you. It has always been you. I just wasn't ready to deserve you back then.

I believe I am now.

The Ask

One more chance. A final one.

I am not going to pretend this is easy to ask. After everything, after all this time, showing up and saying "please, one more chance" takes everything I have. But I would rather be brave enough to ask than spend another five years wondering what could have been.

So here it is, plainly: I want to be with you, Dineo. Not in a rushed, careless way. Not in a way that repeats old mistakes. I mean properly — intentionally, fully, with everything I am now capable of giving. I want to be the person you deserve. I want to show up the way you always showed up for me. I want to build something with you that is steady and warm and honest.

I am asking you for one more chance. A final one. Not because I think I get unlimited tries, but because I believe — deeply — that if you give me this, I will not waste it. I know what I lost. I know what this is worth. And I know that I am ready now in ways I simply wasn't before.

If there is still any part of you that feels something — even a small part, even buried under time and hurt and self-protection — I am asking you to let it speak. Not to me, but to yourself. Ask yourself if what we had was worth giving a proper ending, or a proper beginning.

I am not asking you to forget the past. I am asking you to choose the future, alongside me, with both of us going in clear-eyed and ready. I will earn your trust back, day by day, without making you beg for consistency. I will be the kind of partner you never had to wonder about.

And if you still love me — even a little — please don't let that love go unanswered. You deserve to be with someone who sees you. I see you. I have always seen you. I just need you to let me show you that.

To close

Whatever you decide — thank you for reading this

Dineo, I know this is a lot to take in. And I am not expecting an answer right now, tonight, in the next five minutes. Take whatever time you need. This letter isn't going anywhere, and neither am I.

I just needed you to know how I feel. Fully, without holding back, without hiding behind vague hints or hoping you'd figure it out. You deserve direct. You deserve someone who says what they mean. So I said it.

Whatever you decide, I want you to know something that has nothing to do with what you give me back: your love, the love you showed me five years ago, was one of the most genuine things anyone has ever given me. I did not appreciate it the way I should have. But I have carried it with me every day since. It shaped me. It raised the standard for everything I believed I could be in a relationship. You did that.

So even if you say no. Even if you've moved on completely and there's no room for this conversation in your life — I am grateful. Grateful that I knew you. Grateful that for a time, you chose me. Grateful that your love was the kind that doesn't just disappear — it leaves something behind that makes you a better person.

But I hope, I genuinely hope, that you say yes. I hope you give us this one final chance to do things right. Because I am ready. And I think what we had was worth fighting for — then, and now, and for whatever comes after.

I love you, Dineo Ramaloto. I always have.

— Screw 🌹
